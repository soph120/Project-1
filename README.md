# Global Fuel Price Analysis (AI-Style Insights)

## Overview
This project analyzes global fuel price trends from 2020–2026 using Python. 
It includes data ingestion, exploratory analysis, visualization, and an AI-style insight generation layer.

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle
- Contains: fuel prices, regions, income levels, crude oil prices, tax percentages

## Tech Stack
- Python
- pandas
- matplotlib

## Workflow
- Data Ingestion  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Data Cleaning  
- Feature Engineering & Aggregation  
- Data Visualization  
- AI-Style Insight Generation  

## Key Insights
From 2020 to 2026, average global petrol prices increased from 1.48 to 2.65 USD/L, a net change of 1.16 USD/L (78.3%).
The sharpest annual increase occurred in 2021, when average petrol prices rose by 0.79 USD/L. The largest decline occurred in 2023, with a drop of 0.27 USD/L.
On a regional basis, Europe recorded the highest average petrol price at 3.70 USD/L.
The correlation between Brent crude prices and petrol prices is 0.26, suggesting a weak relationship between crude oil markets and retail fuel prices.
Overall, the data suggests that fuel price movements were shaped by a combination of energy market conditions, possible geopolitical disruptions, and domestic pricing structures such as taxes and subsidies.
If current trends persist, fuel prices may continue to experience upward pressure, particularly in regions with high taxation or limited subsidies.
